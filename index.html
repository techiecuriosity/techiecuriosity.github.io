Welcome to the Dosha Quiz

In Ayurveda, the ancient Indian system of natural healing, health is viewed as a state of balance between mind, body, and spirit. The three doshas—Vata, Pitta, and Kapha—are fundamental energies that govern our physical and mental characteristics, representing unique combinations of the five elements (earth, water, fire, air, and ether).

Vata

Air & Space

The Energy of Movement

Vata Dosha

Vata governs all movement in the mind and body. It controls blood flow, elimination of wastes, breathing and the movement of thoughts across the mind.

Physical Traits:
  • Thin, light frame
  • Quick, graceful movement
  • Dry skin and hair
  • Cold hands and feet
Mental Traits:
  • Creative and imaginative
  • Quick learner
  • Enthusiastic when balanced
  • Anxious when imbalanced

Pitta

Fire & Water

The Energy of Transformation

Pitta Dosha

Pitta governs metabolism, digestion, and energy production. It controls how we process everything from food to thoughts and emotions.

Physical Traits:
  • Medium, athletic build
  • Strong digestion
  • Warm body temperature
  • Sharp features
Mental Traits:
  • Natural leaders
  • Sharp intellect
  • Good decision makers
  • Competitive nature

Kapha

Earth & Water

The Energy of Stability

Kapha Dosha

Kapha governs structure and lubrication in the mind and body. It controls weight, growth, and lubrication for the joints and lungs.

Physical Traits:
  • Strong, solid build
  • Smooth, glowing skin
  • Thick, lustrous hair
  • Regular appetite
Mental Traits:
  • Calm and grounded
  • Excellent memory
  • Loyal and supportive
  • Patient nature

Understanding Vata

Vata is composed of the elements Air and Space. Like the wind, Vata is cool, light, dry, and mobile. In the body, Vata governs movement, including breathing, muscle and tissue movement, heartbeat, and all nerve impulses.

Daily Routine Tips:
  • Maintain regular sleep schedule
  • Practice gentle yoga and meditation
  • Eat warm, cooked meals
  • Stay hydrated with warm beverages
Best Activities:
  • Gentle walking in nature
  • Oil self-massage (Abhyanga)
  • Creative pursuits
  • Mindful breathing exercises

Pitta

The Energy of Transformation

When in Balance:

  • Strong intelligence and focus
  • Natural leadership qualities
  • Efficient digestion

When Out of Balance:

  • Irritability and anger
  • Inflammation and acidity
  • Perfectionism

Understanding Pitta

Pitta is composed of Fire and Water. It governs digestion, metabolism, and transformation in the body and mind. Pitta people are naturally warm, sharp, and focused.

Daily Routine Tips:
  • Exercise during cooler hours
  • Practice moderation in all things
  • Eat cooling, fresh foods
  • Take breaks to prevent overheating
Best Activities:
  • Swimming
  • Moonlight walks
  • Competitive sports
  • Strategic games

Kapha

The Energy of Stability

When in Balance:

  • Strong and stable
  • Calm and compassionate
  • Excellent immunity

When Out of Balance:

  • Lethargy and sluggishness
  • Resistance to change
  • Water retention

Understanding Kapha

Kapha is composed of Earth and Water. It provides structure and lubrication in the body and mind. Kapha individuals are naturally grounded, supportive, and nurturing.

Daily Routine Tips:
  • Wake up early
  • Engage in vigorous exercise
  • Eat light, warm meals
  • Stay mentally stimulated
Best Activities:
  • High-intensity workouts
  • Dance and movement
  • New learning experiences
  • Social activities
